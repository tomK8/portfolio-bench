Audit: raw vs attributed

Raw = sum of every DIVIDEND row for the symbol. Attributed = dividends earned by shares still held (FIFO). Leak = raw − attributed = income that accrued to shares since trimmed or sold — not lost, just no longer driving YoC on the current position. The Attributor shares and Ledger shares columns should match (within 0.01); if not the FIFO engines disagree and one has a bug.

Per-symbol GBP P&L over a date window: end_value − start_value + window_cash_flows, where cash flows sum every ledger row for that symbol (buys negative, sells positive, dividends positive, FX baked in at trade date). Captures realised + unrealised gains plus dividends. Trades at fair value contribute zero — only subsequent market moves and dividends do. Bonds use the per-£100 nominal formula. Symbols with no price_history rows are valued at zero on the boundaries (skewing the row). End-value uses live intraday + live FX when the window ends today, daily close + historical FX otherwise. Return % = P&L ÷ peak GBP capital deployed in the window; Annualised % grosses that up to a 365-day year (so a 2% move over a month reads as ~27%).

Decomposes a stock's price growth into earnings growth vs multiple expansion. TTM diluted EPS comes from SEC EDGAR (XBRL EarningsPerShareDiluted); price from price_history.close. Quarterly EPS records are keyed by period end, the latest filing wins (so restated values are preferred), and each value is rescaled to today's per-share basis using the split factor at its filing date — so prices and EPS share a consistent basis through splits. The series only uses values that were already filed on each chart date. P/E gaps when TTM earnings are negative.
Sharp drops in the chart are usually real, not bugs: NVDA in mid-2023 (AI revenue suddenly doubled, P/E briefly spiked then normalised); AMZN in 2022 (Rivian writedown turned TTM EPS negative). Pre-2018 values for some tickers can be off because XBRL coverage was thinner then. If pre-2022 GOOGL P/E looks off, click Rebuild adj_close on the top of the dashboard — pre-split bars in price_history may have a stale split factor.
